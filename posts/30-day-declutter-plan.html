30-Day Declutter Challenge: Transform Your Home Room by Room

Updated December 2025 • 15 min read

Feeling overwhelmed by clutter? You're not alone. The thought of decluttering an entire home can feel paralyzing—so most people never start.

This 30-day challenge breaks the overwhelming task into small, manageable daily sessions. Just 15-30 minutes a day, one area at a time. By the end of the month, you'll have touched every major area of your home.

The Rules:
  • 15-30 minutes per day maximum
  • Focus only on that day's task
  • Have three bags ready: Trash, Donate, Relocate
  • Done is better than perfect

Before You Start

Gather your supplies:

  • Trash bags
  • Donation boxes or bags
  • A timer (your phone works)
  • This checklist (bookmark this page!)

Week 1: Kitchen & Pantry

1

Expired Food Purge

Check every expiration date in your pantry and fridge. Toss anything expired.

2

Tupperware & Food Storage

Match lids to containers. Recycle anything without a match or that's stained/warped.

3

Utensil Drawer

Remove duplicates (how many spatulas do you really need?). Keep only what you actually use.

4

Under the Sink

Toss old sponges, dried-out cleaners, and mystery bottles. Consolidate duplicates.

5

Spice Cabinet

Spices lose potency after 1-3 years. Smell each one—if it smells like nothing, toss it.

6

Mugs & Glasses

Keep your favorites, donate the rest. Most households need 8-12 mugs max.

7

Junk Drawer

Empty it completely. Sort into categories. Only put back what you actually need.

Week 2: Bathroom & Bedroom

8

Medicine Cabinet

Check expiration dates on all medications. Dispose of expired meds properly.

9

Bathroom Counter & Drawers

Toss old makeup, empty bottles, and products that didn't work for you.

10

Under Bathroom Sink

Consolidate half-empty products. Toss anything you haven't used in 6+ months.

11

Towels & Linens

Keep 2-3 sets per person. Donate old, stained, or threadbare towels.

12

Nightstands

Clear surfaces and drawers. Keep only essentials within arm's reach.

13

Under the Bed

Pull everything out. Only put back seasonal items in proper storage containers.

14

Dresser Drawers

Fold and organize. Donate clothes that don't fit or you haven't worn in a year.

Week 3: Closets & Storage

15

Closet - Hanging Clothes

Turn all hangers backwards. In 6 months, donate anything still turned around.

16

Closet - Shoes

Keep only shoes you've worn in the past year. Toss worn-out pairs.

17

Closet - Accessories

Belts, scarves, bags, jewelry. Keep favorites, donate the rest.

18

Coat Closet

One coat per season per person is usually enough. Donate extras.

19

Linen Closet

Keep 2-3 sheet sets per bed. Donate old bedding to animal shelters.

20

Storage Bins/Boxes

Open every box. If you forgot what's inside, you probably don't need it.

21

Seasonal Items

Holiday decor, seasonal clothes. Consolidate and label clearly.

Week 4: Living Areas & Finish Strong

22

Books & Magazines

Keep only books you'll reread or reference. Donate the rest to your library.

23

Entertainment Center

Old DVDs, video games, cables. If you've gone digital, let the physical go.

24

Paper Clutter

Mail, receipts, documents. Shred what you don't need, file the rest.

25

Kids' Toys (if applicable)

Involve kids if possible. Donate broken toys and ones they've outgrown.

26

Garage/Storage Space

Tools you don't use, broken items, things you're "saving just in case."

27

Digital Declutter

Unsubscribe from emails, delete unused apps, organize your desktop.

28

Car

Trunk, glove box, door pockets. Remove trash and items that belong inside.

29

Donation Drop-off

Take all your donation bags to their final destination. Don't let them linger!

30

Celebrate & Plan Maintenance

Take before/after photos. Set a monthly reminder to do a quick declutter.

What's Next?

Congratulations—you did it! Now the key is maintenance. Schedule a 15-minute weekly tidying session to prevent clutter from building up again.
