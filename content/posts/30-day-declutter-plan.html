Feeling overwhelmed by clutter? You're not alone. The good news: you don't have to tackle it all at once. This 30-day declutter challenge breaks the process into small, manageable daily tasks—each one taking just 15-30 minutes.

By the end of the month, you'll have touched every major area of your home and built habits that actually stick.

How to Use This Challenge

  • Set a daily reminder — Pick a time that works for you (morning, lunch break, after dinner)
  • Grab a timer — Most tasks take 15-30 minutes. Don't overthink it.
  • Have bags ready — Keep trash bags and donation boxes handy
  • No skipping ahead — The tasks build on each other
  • Missed a day? — Just pick up where you left off. No guilt!

Week 1: Quick Wins

We're starting with high-impact, low-effort tasks to build momentum. These are the clutter hotspots everyone has.

Days 1-7

Day 1: Clear the Kitchen Counter

Remove everything. Wipe it down. Only put back what you use daily.

Day 2: Purge the Junk Drawer

Empty it completely. Toss dried pens, mystery keys, expired coupons. Use small containers to organize what stays.

Drawer organizer trays →
Day 3: Medicine Cabinet Cleanout

Check expiration dates on all medications, vitamins, and first aid supplies. Dispose properly.

Day 4: Tackle the Entryway

Sort shoes (donate what you don't wear), organize coats, create a landing zone for keys and mail.

Entryway organizer →
Day 5: Nightstand Declutter

Clear the top. Remove old magazines, empty water bottles, random chargers. Keep only essentials.

Day 6: Bathroom Vanity

Toss old makeup (mascara over 3 months, foundation over 1 year), empty bottles, products you never use.

Day 7: Rest & Donate

Take your donation bags to a drop-off location. Celebrate your first week!

Week 1 Tip: The "maybe" pile is a trap. If you haven't used it in a year and don't love it, let it go.

Week 2: Kitchen & Pantry

The kitchen is the heart of the home—and often the messiest. This week we deep-dive into food storage, cookware, and those forgotten back-of-cabinet items.

Days 8-14

Day 8: Expired Food Purge

Check every item in your pantry, fridge, and freezer. Toss anything expired or freezer-burned.

Day 9: Tupperware & Food Storage

Match lids to containers. Recycle anything warped, stained, or missing its partner.

Container lid organizer →
Day 10: Under-Sink Cabinet

Remove everything. Check for leaks. Organize cleaning supplies with a tension rod or bins.

Under-sink organizer →
Day 11: Spice Cabinet

Toss expired spices (ground spices last 2-3 years). Consolidate duplicates. Consider a tiered organizer.

Spice rack organizer →
Day 12: Pots, Pans & Bakeware

Donate scratched non-stick pans, duplicates, and specialty items you never use.

Day 13: Utensil Drawers

How many spatulas do you really need? Keep favorites, donate the rest.

Day 14: Rest & Donate

Another donation run. You're halfway there!

Week 3: Bedrooms & Closets

Time to tackle the clothes, shoes, and that closet you've been avoiding. Deep breaths—you've got this.

Days 15-21

Day 15: Sock & Underwear Drawer

Toss singles, stretched elastic, holey items. Fold and organize what remains.

Drawer dividers →
Day 16: T-Shirts & Casual Tops

Try on anything you're unsure about. If it doesn't fit or you don't love it, let it go.

Day 17: Pants & Bottoms

Apply the same try-on rule. Donate jeans you're "saving" for later.

Day 18: Dresses, Suits & Formal Wear

Keep what fits and flatters. One or two formal options is usually enough.

Day 19: Shoes

Pull them all out. Donate uncomfortable, worn-out, or duplicate pairs.

Over-door shoe organizer →
Day 20: Accessories (Belts, Scarves, Jewelry)

Untangle, organize, and donate what you never reach for.

Day 21: Rest & Donate

This might be your biggest donation yet. Take a victory lap!

Week 4: Living Spaces & Finishing Strong

The final stretch! We're hitting living areas, paperwork, and tying up loose ends.

Days 22-30

Day 22: Books & Magazines

Keep only books you'll actually reread or reference. Recycle old magazines.

Day 23: DVDs, CDs & Media

In the streaming age, do you need physical media? Donate or sell.

Day 24: Paperwork & Mail

Shred old bills, file important documents, set up a mail system.

Desktop file organizer →
Day 25: Electronics & Cables

Recycle old phones, tangle of mystery cables, outdated chargers.

Day 26: Linens & Towels

Keep 2-3 sets per bed, 2 towels per person. Donate worn or excess items.

Day 27: Garage/Storage Area

Tackle one zone: tools, seasonal decor, or sports equipment.

Day 28: Sentimental Items

The hardest category. Keep what truly matters, photograph the rest.

Day 29: Final Sweep

Walk through every room. Catch anything you missed. Make final donation piles.

✓
Day 30: Celebrate!

Take before/after photos. Treat yourself. You did it!

What's Next?

Congratulations—you just decluttered your entire home! To keep the momentum going:

Weekly Maintenance (10 minutes)

  • Do a quick "reset" of flat surfaces
  • Process incoming mail immediately
  • Return items to their homes before bed
  • One in, one out rule for new purchases
