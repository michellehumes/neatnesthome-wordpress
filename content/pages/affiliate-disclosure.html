The Short Version: Some links on this site are affiliate links. If you click and buy something, we earn a small commission at no extra cost to you. We only recommend products we genuinely believe in.

Full Disclosure

Neat Nest Home is a participant in the Amazon Services LLC Associates Program, an affiliate advertising program designed to provide a means for sites to earn advertising fees by advertising and linking to Amazon.com and affiliated sites.

This means that when you click on certain links on our website and make a purchase, we may receive a small commission from Amazon. This commission comes at no additional cost to you—the price you pay is the same whether you use our link or go directly to Amazon.

Why We Use Affiliate Links

Running a website costs money—hosting, design, research time, and more. Affiliate commissions help us:

  • Keep the site running and ad-free (or with minimal ads)
  • Spend time researching and comparing products
  • Create detailed, helpful content for free
  • Update old posts with current information

Our Promise to You

Honesty First

We will never recommend a product just because it pays a higher commission. Our recommendations are based on research, reviews, and what we believe will genuinely help you.

Full Transparency

We clearly disclose affiliate relationships. When a post contains affiliate links, you'll see a disclosure notice at the top of the article.

Balanced Reviews

We include both pros AND cons in our reviews. No product is perfect, and we'll tell you the downsides so you can make an informed decision.

No Pay-for-Play

Companies cannot pay us to write positive reviews. While we occasionally receive products for review, our opinions are always our own and uninfluenced by free products.

How to Identify Affiliate Links

Affiliate links on our site typically:

  • Link to Amazon.com product pages
  • Are clearly labeled with buttons like "View on Amazon" or "Check Price"
  • Appear in posts with a disclosure notice at the top

If you're ever unsure whether a link is an affiliate link, you can hover over it and look at the URL—Amazon affiliate links contain "tag=" followed by our affiliate ID.

Your Choice

You are never obligated to use our affiliate links. If you'd prefer not to, you can:

  • Search for the product directly on Amazon
  • Copy the product name and paste it into Amazon's search
  • Use a browser extension that removes affiliate tags

We appreciate when you do use our links, as it supports our work at no cost to you, but we completely understand if you choose not to.
